Notice: By the Fundamental Theorem of Asset Pricing, the USD option price remains invariant regardless of the choice of numeraire. Minor variations are purely Monte Carlo sampling noise.

A. The Actual GBM & Discrete Simulation

A variable evolving according to Geometric Brownian Motion follows the SDE: dSt = μ St dt + σ St dWt.

Applying Ito's Lemma to ln(St) yields the discrete Monte Carlo formula for interval Δt:

St+Δt = St exp( (μ - ½σ²)Δt + σ√Δt Z )

B. USD Risk-Neutral Measure (ℚUSD)

Under the domestic risk-neutral measure, the drift μ of a futures contract is zero.

SDE: dSt / St = 0 dt + σS dWtS, USD
Valuation: V0 = e-rUSDT Eℚ_USD [ max(ST - K, 0) ]

C. Change of Numeraire to Foreign Currency (e.g., GBP)

By Girsanov's Theorem, switching the numeraire to the foreign money market account introduces a Quanto Adjustment drift shift to the futures process equal to its covariance with the exchange rate:

SDE: dSt / St = -Cov(S, EFX) dt + σS dWtS, FX
Valuation: V0 = (1 / E0) e-rFXT Eℚ_FX [ max(ST - K, 0) × ET ]
